# Voice Guide

Reference for matching Paul's writing voice in blog posts and other prose.

## Sentence Rhythm

Medium-length, declarative. Subject-verb-object with occasional parenthetical asides in dashes or brackets for context. No choppy fragments, no long compound sentences.

## Tone

Conversational-expert. Talks to peers — doesn't explain what S3 is, but explains *why* a choice was made. Opinions are owned with "I" rather than hidden behind passive voice or stated as universal truths.

## Transitions

Leans on "However" to pivot, and "Bear in mind" or "Remember that" when flagging a nuance. Avoids "Moreover" / "Furthermore" / "Additionally" — a more natural register than that.

## Honesty

Names what didn't work without dwelling on it. Self-deprecation is light and functional, not performative.

## Humour

One cultural reference or wry aside per piece, max. Never forced.

## Structure

Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences). Tables for reference material. Bold for key terms in lists. Front-load the point, then support it.

## Language

British English throughout ("colours", "behaviour", etc.).

## What to Avoid

- No exclamation marks for emphasis
- No "Let's dive in!" openings
- No filler hedging ("it's worth noting that...")
- No selling or hype
- No passive voice where "I" would be more natural
